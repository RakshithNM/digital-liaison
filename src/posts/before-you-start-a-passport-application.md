Applying for a passport usually becomes harder when the problem is not the form itself, but a mismatch across the supporting records. A private assistance service like Sahaay Digital should help people identify those issues **before** they proceed to the official channel.

## What to review first

- Check whether the name format is consistent across PAN, older passport records, address proof, and education documents.
- Confirm the date of birth matches across the documents you expect to rely on.
- Make sure the current address proof is valid and matches the address you plan to use.
- Read the current requirements on the [Passport Seva portal](https://portal2.passportindia.gov.in/AppOnlineProject/welcomeLink).

## A practical way to prepare

1. Decide which category of application applies to you.
2. Identify the core documents you will actually rely on.
3. Check whether initials, expanded names, and spellings match.
4. Resolve obvious conflicts before you book or submit anything official.

> Sahaay Digital does not issue passports or approve applications. Official decisions remain with Passport Seva and the Ministry of External Affairs.

## What not to assume

Do not assume that one familiar ID document automatically fixes a mismatch elsewhere. If a record is materially inconsistent, the safer approach is usually to understand the official requirement first and then correct the underlying issue through the proper channel.
